RecoveryOS

Executive Snapshot

What it is: A neuroadaptive operating system for real-life recovery, routed in the moment and proven with receipts.
Why it wins: Heat-aware, consent-bound delivery that turns micro-interventions into identity updates.
How it runs: Sense the state, route the right primitive, deliver inside the moment, seal with proof, transfer across rooms.
Proof standard: Receipts stack per mindblock, tied to state/aim/dose/target so clinicians see pattern movement.

Why Now

Window: real-time neuroadaptive delivery is now feasible (sensors + edge models + consent-first UX).
Gap: care misses the heat moment when the pattern fires; plans fall apart in the wild.
Edge: RecoveryOS ties state/aim/dose/target to each receipt so it travels across rooms and orgs.
Outcome: safer, repeatable rewiring with auditability clinicians and regulators can trust.

Design Tenets

Safety-first: consent, appropriateness, human boundary before delivery speed.
Minimal moves: smallest runnable primitive with a defined heat band and exit.
Proof-first: every move pairs to a receipt the nervous system and clinician can trust.
Transferable: receipts and IDs travel across rooms, so progress compounds, not resets.
One Surface Rule: one elevated surface in any hierarchy; UI defers to content.
Token Rule: all color/type/space/motion come from tokens; CI/lint guard raw styling.

System Stack

State sensing → Routing → Delivery primitives → Proof capture → Transfer → Governance
Rooms: LUMA orchestrates cues and receipts across Clinic, Self, Relational rooms.
Primitives: NaviCues, Journeys, Mindblocks, Schemas, Proof receipts.
Spine: IDs stay stable; receipts carry state/aim/dose/target and provenance.
Controls: CI locks spine/tokens; proofs stay auditable across rooms.
Outcomes: safer iterations, auditable signal, transferable change.
